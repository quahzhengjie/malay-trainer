title: The passive voice
level: B2

Active: **Saya membaca buku itu.** — I read the book.

**The di- passive.** The `me-` verb becomes a `di-` verb, and the doer (the
"agent") follows the word **oleh**:

> **Buku itu dibaca oleh saya.** — The book was read by me.

**The agent-fronted passive.** When the doer is a pronoun (saya, awak, dia),
Malay usually prefers a tighter pattern — *pronoun + bare verb*, with no `di-`
and no `oleh`:

> **Buku itu saya baca.** — The book, I read.

**The kena passive.** `kena` makes an *adversative* passive — something
unpleasant happened to the subject:

> **Dia kena marah.** — He got scolded.
