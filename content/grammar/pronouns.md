Malay pronouns change with **formality**, not with grammatical case — `saya` is both "I" and "me".

| Person | Formal / neutral | Informal |
|---|---|---|
| I / me | saya | aku |
| you | awak / anda | kau / engkau |
| he / she / him / her | dia | dia |
| we (incl. listener) | kita | kita |
| we (excl. listener) | kami | kami |
| they / them | mereka | mereka |

## Two things beginners must not miss

1. **`kita` vs `kami`.** `kita` *includes* the person you are talking to; `kami` *excludes* them. *Kita makan* = let's eat (you too). *Kami makan* = we ate (not you).
2. **`saya` vs `aku`.** Use `saya` with strangers, elders, and at work. `aku` is only for close friends and family — using it with a stranger sounds rude.

## Possession

Possession is simply the pronoun placed **after** the noun — no extra word needed:

- **buku saya** — my book
- **rumah dia** — his / her house
- **kereta kami** — our car

This is the opposite order from English, so watch for it.
