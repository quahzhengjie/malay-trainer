id: prefix-ber
title: Verbs with ber-
level: A2
tags: [affix, verbs]
---
The **`ber-`** prefix turns a root into an *intransitive* verb — a verb that
does not take a direct object. It usually means "to do an activity" or "to have".

| Root | + ber- | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| jalan | berjalan | to walk |
| lari | berlari | to run |
| cakap | bercakap | to speak / talk |
| main | bermain | to play |
| renang | berenang | to swim |
| kerja | bekerja | to work |
| ajar | belajar | to study / learn |

Two spelling quirks worth knowing:

- Before a root starting with **r**, `ber-` drops its own r: *renang → berenang*.
- *bekerja* and *belajar* are irregular — `ber-` becomes **be-**.
