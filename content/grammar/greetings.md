Malay greetings are built around the word **`selamat`** ("safe / blessed") plus a time word.

| Malay | English | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| Selamat pagi | Good morning | until ~noon |
| Selamat tengah hari | Good afternoon | ~noon to 2pm |
| Selamat petang | Good evening | ~2pm to dusk |
| Selamat malam | Good night | after dark |

## Everyday polite words

- **Terima kasih** — thank you. Reply: **Sama-sama** (you're welcome).
- **Apa khabar?** — How are you? Reply: **Khabar baik** (I'm well).
- **Maaf** — sorry / excuse me.
- **Tolong** — please / help me.
- **Sila** — please (when offering or inviting, e.g. *Sila duduk* = please sit).

> `selamat` shows up beyond greetings too: **Selamat datang** (welcome), **Selamat jalan** (safe journey — said to the person leaving), **Selamat tinggal** (goodbye — said by the person leaving).
