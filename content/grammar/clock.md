Time is built on **`pukul`** ("o'clock") plus a number.

| Malay | English |
|---|---|
| Pukul berapa? | What time is it? |
| pukul satu | one o'clock |
| pukul dua belas | twelve o'clock |
| jam | hour / clock |
| minit | minute |
| saat | second |

**Minutes.** The everyday way is *pukul* + hour + minutes:

- **pukul tiga lima belas** — 3:15
- **pukul tiga tiga puluh** — 3:30

You can also say **pukul tiga setengah** (half past three) and
**pukul empat kurang suku** (a quarter to four) — *setengah* is "half",
*suku* is "a quarter".

Add a time-of-day word for clarity: **pagi** (morning), **tengah hari** (midday),
**petang** (afternoon), **malam** (night) — *pukul lapan pagi* = 8 a.m.
