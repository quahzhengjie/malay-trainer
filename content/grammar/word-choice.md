Advanced Malay is often about picking the *precise* word from a set of
near-synonyms.

**ialah vs adalah** — both link a subject to what follows.

- **ialah** is used before a **noun**: *Ibu kota Malaysia ialah Kuala Lumpur.*
- **adalah** is used before an **adjective** or a **prepositional phrase**:
  *Cadangan itu adalah baik.*

**boleh vs dapat** — both translate as "can".

- **boleh** leans towards *permission* or general possibility: *Awak boleh masuk.*
- **dapat** leans towards *capability* or managing to: *Saya dapat menyiapkannya.*

**mahu / hendak / ingin** — all mean "want".

- **mahu / nak** — plain wanting (*nak* is colloquial).
- **hendak** — wanting, more formal; also "about to".
- **ingin** — to wish or desire; the softest and most refined.
